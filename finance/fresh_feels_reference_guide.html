Fresh Feels — Finance & Ops Reference Guide

Ops Sheet v5 · Commercial Engine v5 · FY26 · Generated 28 March 2026

This document explains both Google Sheets in full — what they are for, the logic each tab works to, what data must be imported or manually entered, and the scripts used to build them. Read this before touching any yellow cells or running any scripts.

System Overview — Two Sheets, One Business

Fresh Feels runs on two separate Google Sheets that serve different purposes and pull from different data sources. They are deliberately kept separate so that operational and financial data each have a clean home.

🔧 Ops Sheet
Manages products, stock, pricing and purchasing. This is the internal operational brain — it tells you what you have, what it costs, when to reorder, and what purchase orders are in flight. Updated by the operations team. Data comes primarily from Shopify (inventory + sales velocity) and is supplemented by manual inputs (COGS, SOH, gifting allocations).
ops_rebuild_v5.js → 9 tabs
📊 Commercial Engine
Tracks financial performance, revenue by SKU, and customer health. This is the commercial dashboard — it shows your P&L by month, which products are driving revenue, and how the AU customer base is behaving. Data comes from Xero (accounting) and Shopify (sales + cohort analytics). All analysis updates automatically from three paste-in RAW tabs — no formulas need touching.
commercial_engine_v5.js → 7 tabs

How the Two Sheets Connect

The two sheets are independent — they do not reference each other via formula. However they share the same SKU code system (FF-[CAT]-[PROD]-[VAR] format), so SKU-level data in the Commercial Engine maps directly to products in the Ops Sheet. The Ops Sheet's OVERVIEW also reminds you to refresh both Shopify exports when you update data.

OPS SHEET v5

Ops Sheet — Products, Pricing, Inventory & Purchasing

Built by: ops_rebuild_v5.js · Run buildAll() to rebuild all 9 tabs from scratch

The Ops Sheet is the operational source of truth for Fresh Feels. It manages the full product lifecycle from cost and pricing through to inventory levels and purchase orders. It does not track financial P&L — that lives in the Commercial Engine. The product registry (PRODUCTS array) is hardcoded inside the script, so adding a new product requires updating the script and re-running buildAll().

Tab-by-Tab Reference

Tab Type Purpose & Logic
OVERVIEW Dashboard 4 live KPI tiles, all formula-driven from other tabs.
  • Active SKUs — COUNTA(SKU MASTER!B5:B24) — counts populated product name rows in SKU MASTER
  • Reorder Flags — COUNTIF(INVENTORY!J5:J30,"⚠ REORDER") — counts red reorder alerts in INVENTORY. Non-zero means stock action needed.
  • Open POs — COUNTIF(PO TRACKER!L4:L50,"Open") — counts purchase orders with status "Open" in PO TRACKER
  • Avg Margin — AVERAGE(COGS & PRICING!P5:P30) — average margin % across all products
Also contains quick navigation links to all tabs and a colour guide reminder.
SHOPIFY_RAW Paste-in Paste destination for the Shopify "Comprehensive Sales by SKU" CSV export. This is the only external data source for the Ops Sheet. Expected columns (matching Shopify's export format): SKU, Month, Total Sales, Quantity Ordered, Orders, Average Order Value, Discounts, Gross Profit, Net Items Sold. The INVENTORY tab reads column D (Quantity Ordered) from this tab via SUMIF to calculate monthly sales velocity per SKU. Paste from row 3 — rows 1–2 are the header block.
SKU MASTER Registry The source of truth for all products and bundles. Every product in the Ops Sheet originates here. Contains three sections:
  • Active Products — 16 products (14 sellable + 2 Proof Packs), each with name, variant, category, SKU code and barcode
  • Add New Products — 5 yellow entry rows. Fill these when launching a new product, then copy the row up to the Active Products section. After adding here you must also add corresponding rows in COGS & PRICING and INVENTORY (yellow rows at the bottom of each), then rebuild via script if doing a full refresh.
  • Bundle SKUs — 12 bundles listed with their FF-BDL-xxx-xxx codes
  • SKU Derivation Key — reference table showing category codes (LND, KTN, BTM, PSC, MLP, ACC, BDL) and variant codes (FNP, SMB, ZYL, SNG, MIX, GFT) used to construct SKUs
COGS & PRICING Manual + Calc Full landed cost and margin analysis for every product. This is the most data-entry-intensive tab — most of the yellow cells live here. The tab works as follows:
  • FX Rate (row 2–3) — live AUD/USD rate via GOOGLEFINANCE("CURRENCY:USDAUD"). A fixed rate override (amber cell F2) lets you lock the rate. Cell I2 ("YES"/"NO") controls which rate is active. All cost calculations use the effective rate in C3.
  • Estimated COGS — built from USD Cost × FX rate + Freight + Duties + C&C (all yellow manual inputs). Shown both ex-GST and inc-GST.
  • Actual COGS (col K) — the most important column. Once goods are landed and all invoices are received, enter the true landed cost (ex GST) here. All margin calculations switch to actual COGS once this is filled.
  • RRP (col M) — pre-populated from the script for standard products. Update for new products or pricing changes.
  • Margin $ and Margin % — calculated as RRP (ex GST) minus COGS (using actual if available, estimated otherwise). This is what hits the OVERVIEW AVG MARGIN tile.
  • Promo tiers (cols Q–V) — automatically calculates selling price and resulting margin at 20%, 30% and 40% discounts. Colour-coded blue/amber/red by tier to show impact at a glance.
⚠️ The two Proof Pack products (FF-LND-LNDST-FNP-PP and FF-KTN-DSWST-ZYL-PP) have $0 RRP in the script — their margin columns will show errors until you enter the correct RRP in col M.
BUNDLES Reference Bundle register and volume discount reference. Lists all 12 bundles with their SKU codes. Also contains the volume discount tier table: Buy 2+ = 20% off, Buy 4+ = 30% off, Buy 6+ = 40% off. AOV target is $60+. Bundle compositions (which component SKUs make up each bundle) are managed in SKU MASTER. Use this tab when setting up discount rules in Shopify or briefing the team on discount policy.
INVENTORY Manual + Calc Stock-on-hand tracking and automated reorder management. This is where operations decisions live. Logic per product row:
  • SOH (col D) — manual yellow input. Total physical units at your 3PL. Update monthly from Shopify Inventory export or 3PL report. Date of last update in cell C3.
  • Gift Alloc (col E) — manual yellow input. Units reserved for influencer gifting over the next 3 months (cross-reference with the GIFTING tab).
  • Sellable SOH (col F) — = SOH − Gift Alloc. Actual units available for sale.
  • Monthly Velocity (col G) — =IFERROR(ROUND(SUMIF(SHOPIFY_RAW!A:A, SKU, SHOPIFY_RAW!D:D) / H3, 0), 0). Looks up all quantity-ordered rows in SHOPIFY_RAW matching this SKU, sums them, then divides by the velocity period (H3, default 3 months) to get average monthly units sold. Auto-updates when you paste new data into SHOPIFY_RAW.
  • Months Cover (col H) — = Sellable SOH ÷ Monthly Velocity. How many months of stock remain at current sell-through rate. Shows "∞" if velocity is 0.
  • Lead Time (col I) — manual yellow input, default 5 months. Time from placing a PO to goods being ready to sell (production + freight + customs).
  • Reorder Flag (col J) — =IF(Months Cover ≤ Lead Time + 1, "⚠ REORDER", "OK"). Triggers when you have less than one month's buffer beyond your lead time. Highlighted red. This count feeds the OVERVIEW Reorder Flags tile.
  • Reorder Qty Suggestion (col K) — = (Lead Time + 3) × Monthly Velocity − Sellable SOH. Suggests how many units to order to cover the lead time plus a 3-month buffer post-arrival.
GIFTING Manual + Calc Annual influencer seeding and gifting plan. No external data source — this is entirely manual and strategic. Logic per product:
  • Annual Demand (col E) — blue strategy input. Your forecast of total units sold this year. Not from Shopify — this is your plan, reviewed quarterly.
  • Seeding % (col F) — blue strategy input. Percentage of annual demand to allocate to gifting (e.g. 0.05 = 5%). Set annually.
  • Annual Seed Units (col G) — = Annual Demand × Seeding %. How many units are earmarked for gifting across the year.
  • Gifted YTD (col H) — yellow manual input. Running total of units physically sent to influencers since 1 July. Update monthly. Keep a separate gifting log with recipient names and dates.
  • Remaining (col I) — = Annual Seed Units − Gifted YTD. Turns red if you have gifted more than planned. Use these figures to set the Gift Alloc column in INVENTORY.
PO TRACKER Manual + Calc Log of all purchase orders — the buying pipeline. Enter every PO here immediately when placed, so that cash flow and lead times are always visible. Columns: PO #, Supplier, Product/SKU, Units, Unit Cost (USD), Total USD, Total AUD, Order Date, Ship Date ETA, ETA Australia, Status, Notes. AUD Total auto-calculates using the live FX rate from COGS & PRICING cell C3 — so it always reflects the current exchange rate. Status workflow: Draft → Placed → In Production → Shipped → In Customs → Received. "Received" rows are greyed out. The Total Open PO Value (AUD) summary at row 55 sums all non-Received rows — this feeds the OVERVIEW Open POs KPI tile (which counts rows with status "Open").
PO TEMPLATE Template Printable purchase order document. Fill in the yellow header fields (PO Number, Supplier, Order Date, Ship Date ETA, Contact, Payment Terms), then enter SKU, quantity and unit cost (USD) for each line item. Total USD and Total AUD auto-calculate. AUD unit cost uses the live FX rate from COGS & PRICING. Up to 20 line items. Once filled, print or export as PDF to send to your supplier. Copy the PO details across to PO TRACKER once placed.

COMMERCIAL ENGINE v5

Commercial Engine — Financial Performance & Customer Analytics

Built by: commercial_engine_v5.js · Run buildAll() to rebuild all 7 tabs from scratch

The Commercial Engine is the financial and commercial view of the business. It has three RAW paste tabs — one from Xero, two from Shopify — and three analysis tabs that calculate everything via formula from those pastes. Once the initial build is done, you never run the script again — just paste fresh exports into the three RAW tabs and every analysis tab updates automatically.

Tab-by-Tab Reference

Tab Type Purpose & Logic
OVERVIEW Dashboard 8 commercial KPI tiles, all formula-driven.
  • Revenue YTD — sum of all monthly revenue in P&L MONTHLY
  • Gross Margin % — YTD Gross Profit ÷ Revenue
  • Net Profit YTD — YTD net profit from P&L MONTHLY
  • Customers (AU) — New Customers figure from CUSTOMERS tab (COHORT_RAW)
  • Total Units Sold — sum of all quantities from SALES BY SKU
  • Total Discounts — sum of discount values from SALES BY SKU
  • Avg Days Since Order — customer recency from COHORT_RAW
  • Avg Order Value — Total Spend ÷ Total Orders from COHORT_RAW
Also contains navigation links and a colour guide note.
XERO_RAW Paste-in Paste destination for the Xero Profit & Loss Monthly export. Xero exports a CSV with account names in column A and one column per month. The P&L MONTHLY tab reads this via SUMIF and INDEX/MATCH, looking up exact account name strings. The account names in Xero must match the list in the script exactly — see the Xero Account Names section below. Paste the full Xero CSV from cell A1, replacing all existing content.
SHOPIFY_SKU_RAW Paste-in Paste destination for the Shopify "Comprehensive Sales by SKU" CSV export. Same report as SHOPIFY_RAW in the Ops Sheet — the two can be the same export pasted in parallel. SALES BY SKU reads quantity ordered and revenue from this tab via SUMIF matching SKU code and month. Month values must be in mmm yyyy format (e.g. "Jan 2026") — Shopify exports in this format by default. A helper column in SALES BY SKU handles the matching. Paste full file from A1.
COHORT_RAW Paste-in Paste destination for the Shopify Cohort Report CSV export. Expected columns: A = Country, B = New Customers, C = Total Spend, D = Total Orders, E = Days Since Last Order, F = % Who Ordered Again. The CUSTOMERS tab uses MATCH("AU", COHORT_RAW!A:A, 0) to find the AU row and reads all 5 metrics from it. Since Fresh Feels only sells in Australia, there will only ever be one AU row — the country breakdown section was removed in v4 for this reason. Paste full file from A1.
P&L MONTHLY Calculated Full Profit & Loss statement, July 2025 – June 2026 (FY26). Each column is one month; column B is YTD Total. Sections: Revenue (Afterpay / PayPal / Shopify), Total Revenue, Cost of Sales (COGS + Warranty), Total COGS, Gross Profit, Gross Margin %, Operating Expenses (22 line items), Total OpEx, Net Profit/(Loss), Net Margin %. All values pull from XERO_RAW via SUMIF formulas that match the exact Xero account name. Gross Margin % and Net Margin % show "—" (not errors) when revenue is zero — this is by design.
SALES BY SKU Calculated Revenue, units, gross profit and discounts broken down by SKU across FY26 months. 14 sellable SKUs are tracked (Proof Packs excluded). Four sections, each with the same product list and monthly columns:
  • Revenue (Total Sales $AUD) — monthly revenue per SKU plus "No SKU / Unassigned" catch-all row and TOTAL
  • Units Sold (Qty Ordered) — units ordered per SKU per month
  • Gross Profit $ (Shopify-reported) — Shopify's own gross profit figure (requires COGS to be set up in Shopify admin). Note: this is Shopify's view, not the Ops Sheet COGS calculation.
  • Discounts Applied $AUD — discount value given per SKU per month (negative = discount to customer)
All values read from SHOPIFY_SKU_RAW via SUMIF matching SKU code × month.
CUSTOMERS Calculated AU customer health snapshot from the Shopify Cohort Report. Two sections:
  • 3 KPI tiles — Total Customers (AU), Total Orders (AU), Avg Days Since Order. Interpreted as: under 60 days = healthy repeat purchase rate, over 90 days = churn risk worth actioning.
  • Customer Behaviour — AU (6 metrics) — New Customers, Total Spend, Total Orders, Avg Days Since Order, % Who Ordered Again (repeat purchase rate), Avg Order Value. All read from the AU row of COHORT_RAW. These are the primary retention metrics to track month-over-month.
All six metrics use IFERROR(INDEX(COHORT_RAW!col, IFERROR(MATCH("AU", COHORT_RAW!A:A, 0), 2)), fallback) — the MATCH finds the AU row dynamically, so it doesn't rely on AU always being in a fixed row position.

Data Required — Commercial Engine

📥 Import 1 — XERO_RAW: Xero → Reports → Profit and Loss → set date range to 1 Jul 2025 – 30 Jun 2026 → Monthly view → Export CSV. Paste full file from cell A1 in the XERO_RAW tab, replacing all existing content.
Refresh frequency: monthly after books are reconciled
📥 Import 2 — SHOPIFY_SKU_RAW: Shopify → Analytics → Reports → Comprehensive Sales by SKU → set date range to cover FY26 → Export CSV. Paste full file from cell A1 in the SHOPIFY_SKU_RAW tab. Same report used for Ops Sheet SHOPIFY_RAW.
Refresh frequency: monthly (or whenever you want current SKU revenue figures)
📥 Import 3 — COHORT_RAW: Shopify → Analytics → Reports → Cohort Report → Export CSV. This covers rolling 12 months of customer cohort data. Paste full file from cell A1 in the COHORT_RAW tab.
Refresh frequency: monthly for a current customer health snapshot
💡 There are no yellow manual input cells in the Commercial Engine. Every value is either pasted from a RAW tab or calculated by formula. If a number looks wrong, check the relevant RAW tab first.

Xero Account Names — Must Match Exactly

The P&L MONTHLY tab matches account names from the XERO_RAW export using exact string matching. If Xero renames an account (or if you use a different Chart of Accounts), the P&L formula will return $0 for that line. The accounts the script expects are:

Revenue

Sales - Afterpay Sales - PayPal Sales - Shopify

Cost of Sales

Cost of Goods Sold Warranty - Claims

Operating Expenses

Advertising - Facebook Advertising - Google Bank Fees Cleaning Consulting & Accounting Entertainment Filming & Photography Freight & Courier Marketing Merchant Fee - Afterpay Merchant Fee - PayPal Merchant Fee - Shopify Office Expenses Realised Currency Gains Recharges Wages Rent Samples / Mold Costs Sub-Contractor Subscriptions Telephone & Internet Unrealised Currency Gains Warehouse Expenses Waste Disposal Website Development
⚠️ If Xero shows $0 for a P&L line you know has a balance, first check that the account name in your Xero Chart of Accounts matches the exact string above — including spaces, capitalisation and punctuation.

Scripts & Build Process

Both sheets are built by Google Apps Script files stored in your Finance folder. Run buildAll() when you need a clean rebuild — for example, when the script is updated, when a new version is released, or when setting up the sheets for the first time. You do not need to re-run the script for data refreshes — that is handled by pasting into the RAW tabs.

⚠️ buildAll() wipes and rebuilds all tabs. For the Ops Sheet this means re-entering your COGS costs, SOH figures and PO data. For the Commercial Engine it means re-pasting your three RAW exports. Always note down your current yellow-cell values before running a rebuild.
