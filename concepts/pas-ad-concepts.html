01
The Half-Used Bottle
Laundry UGC Reel 15-30s IG + FB
0-3 sec
Hook
"How many half-used detergent bottles do you have under your sink right now?"
P / Problem

Most people have two or three half-used laundry bottles sitting under the sink. You keep buying new ones before the old ones are finished.

A / Agitate

That's money already spent sitting there going to waste. Heavy bottles that spill. Never knowing how much to use. Running out at the worst time.

S / Solve

Fresh Feels laundry sheets are pre-measured. One sheet per wash. No bottles, no measuring, no waste. 80 washes in a box smaller than a cereal packet.

Suggested Script

"How many half-used detergent bottles are under your sink right now? [holds up two or three bottles] That's money just sitting there. I switched to Fresh Feels laundry sheets and genuinely cannot believe I waited this long. Pre-measured, no mess, and this little box does 80 washes. Link in bio."

Visual Direction
  • Real home laundry cupboard, not styled
  • Hold up actual old bottles (the more the better)
  • Reveal Fresh Feels box alongside the mess
  • Show sheet dissolving in machine drum
Execution Notes
  • Genuinely messy cupboard works better than a tidy one
  • Creator should be real and slightly exasperated, not polished
  • The reveal moment is the payoff, give it a beat
  • Caption mirrors the hook line
Stop buying bottles. Link in bio.
Works well as a Reel with text overlay. Scale if it gets strong organic hold rate.

How Many Scoops?
Laundry Brand-produced Reel 15s IG + FB
0-3 sec
Hook
"Quick question. Do you actually know how much detergent to use?"
P / Problem

Nobody measures detergent properly. Everyone guesses. The lines on the cap are confusing and nobody trusts them.

A / Agitate

Use too much and you're wasting money and leaving residue on clothes. Use too little and the load does not come clean. You just pour until it looks right and hope for the best.

S / Solve

Fresh Feels laundry sheets are already measured. One sheet equals one load. Nothing to pour. Nothing to guess. Drop it in the drum and start the machine.

Suggested Script / VO

"Do you actually know how much detergent to use? [show confusing measuring cap] You're either using too much and wasting money, or not enough and the load's not clean. Fresh Feels sheets are pre-measured. One sheet, one load. Drop it in. Done."

Visual Direction
  • Close-up of confusing measuring lines on detergent cap
  • Cut to hand dropping one Fresh Feels sheet into drum
  • Clean, simple. No clutter.
  • Works without sound with text overlays
Execution Notes
  • Short and punchy. 15 seconds max.
  • Works well as a brand-produced cut or UGC style
  • The confusing cap is universally relatable. Everyone has felt this.
Stop guessing. Link in bio.
Strong broad-audience hook. Relatable to anyone who does laundry.

0-3 sec
Hook
"Why are dishwasher pods still wrapped in plastic?"

Traditional dishwasher pods come in single-use plastic packaging inside a giant plastic tub. It is the default and nobody questions it.

A / Agitate

A new tub every few weeks. The plastic wrapper on the pod supposedly dissolves but does not always. The tub is huge, annoying to store and annoying to throw out. You're paying for the packaging as much as the product.

S / Solve

Fresh Feels dishwasher sheets. Fold one in half, drop it in the tray or at the bottom of the machine, close the door. No plastic. Pre-measured. Cleans properly.

Suggested Script / VO

"Why are dishwasher pods still wrapped in plastic? A new tub every few weeks, plastic wrapper that barely dissolves, the whole thing is bigger than it needs to be. Fresh Feels dishwasher sheets. Fold one in half, drop it in. No plastic. Pre-measured. Done."

Visual Direction
  • Hold up a pod with the plastic wrapper clearly visible
  • Then show a Fresh Feels sheet next to it
  • Drop the sheet in the machine. Close the door.
  • Open dishwasher to clean sparkling dishes at the end
Execution Notes
  • The plastic wrapper on standard pods is the visual proof point. Show it clearly.
  • Do not lecture on sustainability. Let the visual do the work.
  • Keep it fast. The problem is obvious. Get to the solve quickly.
Make the swap. Link in bio.
Good awareness-level creative. Broad appeal. Strong for cold audiences.

  • The "flip" framing is strong. Creator should feel like they're letting you in on something.

Hook

Buying cleaning products one at a time is the most expensive way to do it. Constant small purchases add up to more than people realise.

A / Agitate

Most brands push subscriptions or "save when you subscribe" pressure tactics that lock you in. There is a better way that keeps you in control.

S / Solve

Stock Up and Save. Buy 2 or more and save 20%. Buy 4 or more and save 30%. Buy 6 or more and save 40%. Mix anything across the range. No subscription. No lock-in. Just smarter buying.

Slide Copy

Slide 1: "The more you buy, the less each clean costs." / Brand hero
Slide 2: "Buy 2+ items: save 20%. Buy 4+ items: save 30%. Buy 6+ items: save 40%." / Clean typography
Slide 3: "Mix anything across the range. No codes. No catch. Just better value." / Product flatlay
Slide 4: "No subscriptions. No lock-ins. You stay in control." / CTA

Visual Direction
  • Clean, typographic carousel. Brand colours.
  • Product flatlay showing the full range in slide 3
  • Bold, confident numbers. No small print energy.
Execution Notes
  • No urgency or pressure language. The value speaks for itself.
  • Avoid "SALE" framing. This is how the model works, not a promotion.
  • Works well as a retargeting ad for people who have visited the site
